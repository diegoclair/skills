# Hook formulas — pick the right slide 1

> When to read: before drafting the slide-1 `hook` of a carousel.

The slide 1 has 1–2 seconds to stop the scroll. There are 13 hook formulas that account for the vast majority of viral carousels. Pick one that matches the topic; the linter doesn't enforce a specific formula but the `hook_formula` field is recorded for tracking.

Each formula below: pattern, when to use, 2 examples, and the worst common mistake.

## Decision tree — narrow before you browse

Before reading all 13, narrow with 4 questions. Most carousels collapse into 2-3 candidates from this:

```
Do you have a real number you can quantify?
├─ YES, a specific measurable outcome  →  H-01  (Number + Specific Result)
│                                          H-07  (Number + Pain Statement)
│                                          H-13  (Listicle with pain-point spoiler)
│
Do you have a personal experience to confess?
├─ YES, a mistake or transformation     →  H-03  (Confessional / Error)
│                                          H-11  (Before/After Transformation)
│
Do you have a counterintuitive opinion?
├─ YES, going against received wisdom   →  H-02  (Contrarian)
│                                          H-10  (Negation Reveal)
│
None of the above (educational, framework, generic)?
└─                                       →  H-04  (How-To Promise)
                                            H-08  (Mistake-Driven)
                                            H-12  (Question-Inversion)
```

**Tie-breakers between candidates in the same branch:**
- If you're an unknown account, prefer the formula with the **specific number** — proof beats voice when you have no audience.
- If you're an established voice, prefer the formula that sounds the most **like you would say it aloud** — voice beats novelty when you have an audience.
- If the topic is data/B2B/SaaS, see [reference/examples.md — Fishkin](examples.md#reference-3) — his "headline finding before methodology" inversion fits H-01 or H-07 with the value-bomb baked into slide 1.
- If the topic is a personal lesson/mistake, see [Alić's Dear Son carousel](examples.md#reference-1) — confessional + reframe is his strongest pattern.

Now read the formula entries for the 2-3 candidates you narrowed to. Don't read all 13.

## H-01 — Number + Specific Result
> `[N] [object] that [measurable result]`

**Use when:** You can quantify outcome (followers, revenue, time saved, retention).

- "7 mistakes that cost you 10 clients/month"
- "I grew from 0 to 50K followers in 8 months. Here's the exact system."

**Don't:** Use vague results ("lots of clients", "your growth"). Hook dies without a number.

---

## H-02 — Contrarian / Counterintuitive
> `[Accepted truth] actually [negates or inverts]`

**Use when:** The user has a counterintuitive POV that goes against received wisdom in the niche.

- "Posting daily hurts you (do less, better)"
- "Why posting daily HURTS your growth"

**Don't:** Fake contrarianism. If your slide 2–4 doesn't deliver the inversion with evidence, the audience punishes the bait.

---

## H-03 — Confessional / "I Tested"
> `I [did X for N time]. Here's what I learned.`

**Use when:** You have personal experience (failure or experiment) to share.

- "I lost 8 clients in 60 days. Here's what I learned."
- "I posted 100 carousels in 90 days. Here's what actually moves the needle."

**Don't:** Fake the experience. Audiences sniff it out fast.

---

## H-04 — Question that Creates Cognitive Gap
> `[Question the ideal follower CAN'T answer themselves]`

**Use when:** There's a specific question that nags the audience that you have data on.

- "Do you know why people stop on your slide 1?"
- "Why do some carousels get 10× more saves than others?"

**Don't:** Use questions whose answer is obvious ("Do you want more clients?").

---

## H-05 — Specific Promise ("Save This")
> `Save this post: [specific deliverable]`

**Use when:** Carousel is a tangible deliverable (checklist, template, framework).

- "Save this: full checklist for a carousel that converts (7 slides)"
- "Save this: 30 days of outfit ideas (no new clothes needed)"

**Don't:** Promise vague value ("save this post"). Specify the deliverable.

---

## H-06 — Cliffhanger / Incomplete Narrative
> `[Start of dramatic situation] → [resolution at slide 2+]`

**Use when:** Story has tension that only resolves later.

- "I got fired last week. What happened next →"
- "Wait until slide 7. You won't believe it."

**Don't:** Open a loop you don't close. Audience hates dropped cliffhangers.

---

## H-07 — Borrowed Authority / Research Data
> `[Surprising statistic]. But only if you do [X].`

**Use when:** You have a study, dataset, or analysis to reference.

- "I analyzed 500 profiles. Here's what 9/10 do the same."
- "Carousels get 1.4× more reach than single images. But only if you do this."

**Don't:** Invent statistics. The "fragile number" anti-pattern (AP-11) destroys credibility.

---

## H-08 — Negation of Expectation ("The X Most People Miss")
> `What nobody tells you about [topic]`

**Use when:** Niche has a widely-known piece of advice that's wrong or incomplete.

- "The mistake that flops your posts (and you don't even notice)"
- "The #1 reason your Reels get zero views in 2026"

**Don't:** Use this when the audience is at expert level — they will know what you're going to say.

---

## H-09 — Unexpected Comparison / Analogy
> `[Object A] should work like [unexpected Object B]`

**Use when:** Concept needs a fresh frame to land.

- "Your carousel should work like a Netflix trailer, not a Wikipedia article"
- "Your carousel should work like a Netflix trailer, not a Wikipedia article"

**Don't:** Pick analogies the audience won't know.

---

## H-10 — "Stop Doing This" / Negative Command
> `Stop [common behavior]. Do [alternative].`

**Use when:** Behavior is widespread and you can prescribe a clear alternative.

- "Stop using gradients on your carousels"
- "Stop making these 7 LinkedIn mistakes (Number 4 is killing your reach)"

**Don't:** Tell people to stop doing things without an alternative — feels nagging.

---

## H-11 — "Before I Knew This" / Personal Transformation
> `Before [learning], I was [bad state]. Today [good state].`

**Use when:** Transformation has a clear before/after with timeframe.

- "Before I learned this, every carousel took me 3 hours"
- "Before I learned this framework, every carousel took me 3 hours"

**Don't:** Skip the "before" specifics. Without the dark contrast, the after looks unearned.

---

## H-12 — Permission Slip / Pressure Relief
> `You don't need to [thing everyone says is mandatory]`

**Use when:** Niche has a "must do every day" pressure that you can refute.

- "You don't need a designer to have a carousel that converts"
- "You don't need to post every day to grow on Instagram"

**Don't:** Use this if you don't have a real argument behind it. Sounds lazy.

---

## H-13 — Numbered List with "Pain Spoiler"
> `[N] [objects] ([qualifier that hints one will hurt])`

**Use when:** Listicle format AND you have one item that's notably worse than the others.

- "7 mistakes that kill your carousel (#4 will hurt)"
- "5 carousel mistakes killing your engagement (number 4 is the worst)"

**Don't:** Promise pain that doesn't materialize. If item #4 is bland, the trick backfires.

---

## Mechanics that apply to every hook

- **≤12 words.** Hard rule. Linter C1 blocks render above 12.
- **No introduction.** No "Hey everyone, today we're going to talk about…". The hook IS the headline.
- **Contrast ≥7:1** between hook color and background. Thumbnail must be readable when compressed in the feed.
- **Swipe indicator.** Add a `sub: "swipe →"` or arrow visible in the cover layout — improves swipe-through rate by ~9%.
- **Caption starts with the hook.** When you copy `caption_seed` for the post caption, start it with the same hook text — the Instagram algorithm indexes captions for Explore.
